Design Philosophy

A common question that is often dicussed within my degree is "This is a solution, but what is the problem?" Designers often gravitate towards solutioning right away - designing new interfaces, interactions, processes, and experiences - without considering the problem's context. Understanding why there is a problem in the first place is the first step towards a useful solution.

I love the question Why? It's simple, but insightful. "Why did someone do this?" or "Why is this not a thing?" points to the exact cause of a shortcoming. This then leads to my second favorite question How? Again, simple, but this time, actionable. It is why for every project in my portfolio, the first thing I describe is a question. It helps give context and meaning to my project where my designs are not just interesting to look at, but actual serve a greater purpose.

Once a problem is defined, then the ideas can start flowing. I can rev the cogs in my brain and ignite my imagination, but stay grounded with my problem statement to ensure that I am not only desiging for myself, but for anyone who wants to use/observe/interact/expand upon my ideas.
