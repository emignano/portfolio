The Incomplete City

Course: UT 103 The Incomplete City

Professor: Toban Shadlyn

Date: Spring 2022

Project Type: Group

Summary: The Incomplete City project was a course designed around learning about disparate urban systems and how they interact with each other. Modeled after the game Cities: Skylines, our cohort was tasked to create a paper city for a randomly generated stakeholder population where we could only use pictures of urban objects our cohort collected during our Chicago trip. This project was split into three parts:

  • Part One: Create a neighborhood for the assigned stakeholder group

  • Part Two: Combine with 1 or 2 more neighborhoods to create districts

  • Part Three: Combine all the districts together to create a city

The main constraint was that all the city systems had to connect to each other across the entire city, meaning if one group had a light rail system, then we had to incorporate it throughout the remaining districts. Below are some pictures of the entire process and end result.
